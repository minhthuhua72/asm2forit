Project Idea

Overview


Realizing that there is a lack of knowledge about recycling in Vietnam, I have come up with an idea to incorporate waste management into mobile applications, which will be available for cross-platform, both Android and iOS. On the whole, the aim of this project is to develop a real-time 2D objects detection on a reactive smartphone app that will quickly identify and categorize recycling trash. After that, the app tells the users to know which trash bins will be used to throw rubbish away. There are also features to enhance users' experiences, including a QR code scanner in case of the object detection cease to function, a record of recycling activities, and a list of recycling locations based on the phone GPS.

Motivation


First of all, I have studied abroad in Calgary, Canada for three years and I remembered how amazingly their waste-sorting robot worked at the mall. In particular, the recycling robot is known as Oscar, which used artificial intelligence to classify trash into the corresponding waste bins. As a result, the idea has come to my mind that it will be very utilitarian if we have a smartphone app that works pretty much like Oscar. Secondly, according to the Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment, each family in Vietnam uses up to one kilogram of plastic bags each month. Therefore, big cities like Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City can discharge up to 80 tons of plastic trash into the environment. Moreover, the majority of trash will be buried or sent to landfills due to the inefficient classification recycling trash, in which people have difficulties to remember and figure out the suitable bins for each type of garbage. Furthermore, there are some cases that the trash does not fit in any category illustrated on the trash bin, which leads to people either mixing up the trash or throwing them in the random waste carts. As a consequence, these actions have ended up with a polluted and contaminated environment as it will be very time-consuming to sort trash out. Hence, I believe this project will simplify and save time on the process of recycling. In addition, it will have a positive impact on the environment because the more people use the app, the more garbage will be recycled, and the project also offers to acknowledge people about waste disposal and what they can do to protect the environment.



Description


The first and foremost feature I would like to develop is a real-time object detection that is used to differentiate garbage and sort them into the following categories: organic waste, recyclable, and non-recyclable. In specific, the feature mainly focuses on dealing with 2D objects because 3D detection can be very challenging to carry out as there is a lack of diverse datasets within a category. In addition, this feature is significantly inspired by the iPhone's camera which has a good reputation for its face recognition so I think that it will be a brilliant idea to incorporate object detection into smartphone applications. Therefore, people will be able to recognize the recycling trash and highlight their characteristics in real time. For instance, say you buy a cup of iced coffee from Highlands. It is obvious that they will serve your beverage in a cup with a lid and a straw, all in plastics. However, after you have finished drinking, you might not know if everything you use is recyclable so you use the object detection via camera feed and it will return with the result like "Recyclable but remember to remove the liquid". At the same time, the screen background will turn blue to tell you the respective bin which is for recycling trash. As a result, you know exactly where to throw your trash away and save more time rather than looking left and right to locate the right waste bins. Overall, a real-time 2D object detection is expected to work by training through an open-source software library for machine learning like Google Tensorflow, which is based on the proposed paperworks like Scaled-YOLOv4: Scaling Cross Stage Partial Network.

The second feature of the product that I would like to build is a QR code scanner. What I expect from the input is that every item that we use daily will have a QR code printed on the objects where it is the most visible, such as on the milk container’ package or the bottle’s cap. The QR code provides further information about materials that make up the product and whether it is recyclable or not. Therefore, whenever you want to dispose of the trash, all you need to do is use this feature to scan and read the code, then the app displays information on the screen either "Recyclable" or "Non-recyclable". Subsequently, the application changes the colour of its background corresponding to the colour of the waste bin. Additional information to colours of trash cans, green representing organic wastes, such as food or drink, whereas blue stands for recycling bin, and black bins are used for the remaining rubbish. This feature is an alternative to the 2D objects detection in case of malfunctions and it only works best for those items that can be printable on the packaging. Let’s take an example of a can of Coca-Cola having a QR code apparently displayed on the surface. By scanning the QRcode, users are able to indicate whether the can is recyclable or not, and acquire further information about materials made up of this product. In this case, the app will let the users know that the Coca-Cola can is recyclable because it is made of 42% recycled aluminum and 56% new aluminum. As a result, it is now the users’ duty to throw the can into the right bin corresponding to the recyclable.

The third feature I would like to add to the project is a dashboard called "Your activity". In essence, it keeps track of the users’ activities and informs the number of times people use the app daily and the average amount of disposed waste each day. The data will depend on QR codes users scan or how many times the object detection succeeds to identify recycling trash. Later on, the app can aggregate data to give users an insight into what extent they have done to save the environment. Similar to when you bring your own bottle to get water from the fountain at RMIT, it tells you how many disposable plastic bottles you have saved through using it. I believe that this feature will motivate people to become more involved in recycling because it gives people a sense of reward by showing their accomplishment to save the environment.

Last but not least, this project will include a list of nearby recycling centers, which are based on the phone location. Therefore, it is impossible if there is no GPS involved which will be used to identify the exact location. This feature mainly focuses on detecting the specialized centers dealing with hazardous wastes, like harmful chemicals, or drop-off recycling centers for electronics. This is because it requires a more complicated process to actually recycle these types of items. For example, batteries which are commonly used for everyday gadgets like TV remotes. These batteries are recyclable, however, they need to hand in to specialists of recycling electronics because batteries made of cobalt and lead, which can contaminate the Earth if they are not disposed properly. Therefore, it is very important for users to use first and second features to identify the recyclable garbage and figure out where to send them to.

Tools & Technologies


As mentioned above, this smartphone app will be developed on the cross platform, both Android and iOS, which means that there will be two programming languages involved, in which Android apps will use Java programming language, whereas Swift is used for iOS. Therefore, different softwares can be installed in order to create this app. On the one hand, Android Studio is a system software that is used to build any Android apps and the most important thing to know that it is an open source, which means it is available for any developers to download and work on many platforms. On the other hand, Xcode is the perfect software to write Swift and build an iOS app, however, it can only be installed on Apple's operating system. In other words, even though Xcode is free and open source, it is only available to download from Mac App Store. Besides, in order to build a 2D object detection, it is inevitable to involve machine learning like Google Tensorflow software to train models.

Required Skills


Based on the current skills and the minimal knowledge about artificial intelligence along with machine learning that I have, I believe that carrying out this project will be very challenging and somewhat not feasible. Therefore, I have broken down into smaller tasks that I am able to do in order to increase the practicability of this project. The foremost skill that I need is cross-platform app development in which it is essential to learn Java and Swift programming languages in order to turn my ideas into reality. This can be done through program electives at RMIT such as mobile application development and I expect that this course will cover the open-source softwares supporting those programming languages. Moreover, I find that there are some relevant skills, including database management and UI/UX, also offered by RMIT program electives so it is not really difficult to acquire these skills, yet they will consume lots of time to master. Furthermore, it is essential for me to become proficient in mathematics and statistics to deal with algorithms due to the involvement of machine learning in this project. It is also important for me to understand the process of training a model, in which I need to establish the development environment, collect training datasets together with test data, configure and train the model, convert the trained model to TensorFlow, and eventually test the model in a mobile application.

Outcome


The most significant outcome of this development is its positive impact on the environment, which not only influenced people like us, but also the natural habitat of animals. The more trash that can be recycled, the fewer toxic chemicals and greenhouse gases released to the environment, which slows down global warming so that the habitats of polar bears or penguins will be preserved. In addition, while people are able to minimize the exploitation of raw materials by reprocessing products with recycling materials, the rainforests will be saved from deforestation. For people like us, there will be a save of space owing to the fewer landfills existing and we could save more money on manufacturing products as raw materials cost more than the recycling material and they might be only used once. As a result, this project has a majority of positive effects on human lives and society, besides the time investment to develop a perfectly-working 2D object detection, which can also be used to leverage 3D object detection.
